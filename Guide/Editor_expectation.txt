The book is intended to be a “how to” manual.

In the case of the Pangenomes chapter, I suggest an introduction that provides the rationale for creating pangenomes, some discussion of the utility of pangenomes in rats (pangenomes from inbred (haploid) strains, recent genetic bottlenecking in the wild and even more recent in the lab, ability to capture a large fraction of the existing variation in a single pangenome, utility for examining causative variation mapped in heterogenous stocks whose founders could be included in a pangenome, and other things you will think of). Then a brief  (paragraph level) description of the mathematical basis of a pangenomes and graph theory etc. Then perhaps a survey of the available tools (probably pggb and MG/Cactus will be the focus, but you have scope to include others). Then some discussion of resources required to generate a pangenome graph (HPC requirements, genome assemblies, computational obstacles). Then a discussion of using pangenomes as a resource for mapping, structural and simple variant identification, and other genetic investigation. Visualization is obviously a topic that could benefit from both a discussion and examples of what ODGI and other tools can provide, perhaps some mention of the difficulty of providing on the fly dynamic visualizations. And maybe a discussion of the challenges in exploring large complicated data sets and how that may be addressed in future.

Hope that helps…..of course you have a lot of flexibility….what we would like the reader to know is what use pangenomes are and how they might access this as a research utility in a practical way….
